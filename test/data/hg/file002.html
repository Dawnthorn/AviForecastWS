Avalanche bulletin for the Chic-Chocs

Area concerned:Mont Albert, mont Hog’s Back, Champs-de-Mars, mont Lyall, mont Vallières-de-Saint-Réal, mont Blanche-Lamontagne and Mines-Madeleine.

Issued on : Friday December 12th 2014 at 7:30
Valid until : Sunday December 14th 2014 at 7:30

Danger ratings Friday Saturday Outlook Sunday
Alpine LowLowLow
Treeline LowLowLow
Below treeline LowLowLow

Avalanche problem #1 : None

Storm that hit Gaspe Peninsula Wednesday and Thursday has left less precipitation than expected. Mild temperatures promoted settlement of the snowpack. LOW danger rating does not mean ZERO risk. Use caution on isolated terrain features where a fall can take you towards non-burried obstacles and cause serious injuries.

Avalanche activity

No new avalanche have been observed or reported. Your observations are essential to write avalanche bulletins, so do not hesitate to share them with us at info@centreavalanche.qc.ca or with this online form.

Snowpack :

Last storm left 10-15 cm of moist snow before bringing liquid precipitations and warm temperatures. Result at treeline: snow surface is very cohesive, due to its high water content and rest on a 1 cm thick crust, still not totally frozen. Beneath, fairly dry snow is found on protected areas. Alpine winds have blown off the remaining snow of NE-E-SE aspects, leaving December 1 crust bare which makes travel quite dangerous. On average, the snowpack varies between 30 to 90 cm, depending on aspect and elevation. 

Weather forecast:

Warm temperatures are expected for the entire period of the bulletin and will  fluctuate near the freezing point, even at night. Few precipitations are forecasted Friday afternoon and Saturday morning: rain (5 mm) in the valley and snow (5 cm) in the mountains. Freezing levels will be approximately 1300 m during the day and should not drop to the valley before Sunday night. Winds will be light (variable) down the valley and moderate (25 to 40 km/h) from East up the mountains. A clearing is expected Sunday and should bring some period of sun.

Message:

Did you recently practice your avalanche transceivers skills ? At the beginning of the season, is it time to review and practice companion rescue skills. You can make a difference in your group.

Disclaimer : This avalanche bulletin is prepared with the most recent weather, snowpack and avalanche activity data available for the Chic-Chocs. It is strictly information that can be used as a part of a decision making process and is not intended to be a replacement for knowledge about avalanche safety including and not limited to safe travel practices and the use of backcountry rescue equipment such as beacon, shovel and probe.
