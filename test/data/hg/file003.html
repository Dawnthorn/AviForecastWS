Avalanche bulletin for the Chic-Chocs

Area concerned:Mont Albert, mont Hog’s Back, Champs-de-Mars, mont Lyall, mont Vallières-de-Saint-Réal, mont Blanche-Lamontagne et Mines-Madeleine.

Issued on : Tuesday 28 January 2014 à 7:30
Valid until : Thursday 30 January 2014 à 7:30

Danger ratings Tuesday Wednesday Outlook Thursday
Alpine ConsiderableModerateModerate
Treeline ConsiderableModerateModerate
Below treeline ModerateModerateModerate

Avalanche problem #1 : Wind slabs

The 35cm of snow received since Sunday were transported by strong to extreme S winds (gusting to 220km/h Monday pm) and then W . There will be certain to encounter in the alpine and at treeline thick and hard wind slabs near terrain features of N to E aspects. Softer wind slabs could also be present below treeline. In all cases, these important accumulations could produced avalanches large enough to bury a skier.

Avalanche problem #2 : Persistent slab

Faceted grains present on the January 14th crust could still be activated with the cold and the additional load applied by the accumulation of new snow blown by the wind. Present at all altitudes, this weak layer could produce avalanches larger than those described above .
