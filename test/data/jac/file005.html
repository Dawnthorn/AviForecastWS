Today's Discussion

The National Weather Service Forecasts-

TODAY...PATCHY DENSE FOG WITH VISIBILITY ONE QUARTER
MILE OR LESS AT TIMES NEAR THE AIRPORT. PERIODS OF RAIN. HIGHS
AROUND 38. EAST WIND 10 MPH.

TONIGHT...RAIN MIXING WITH SNOW IN THE EVENING. SNOW ACCUMULATION TO 2 INCHES...HIGHEST AMOUNTS IN MENDENHALL VALLEY AND NORTH OF AUKE BAY. LOWS 32 TO 38. SOUTHEAST WIND 10 MPH.

THURSDAY...RAIN IN THE MORNING...THEN RAIN SHOWERS LIKELY IN THE AFTERNOON. SNOW LEVEL 900 FEET. HIGHS AROUND 38. SOUTHEAST WIND 10 MPH.

Temperatures remain warm with the Tram Summit at +2c and Eaglecrest's summit right at 0c.

Winds have been light to moderate with 10-25mph winds at the tram summit over the last 24 hours and slightly lower along the channel out of our traditional SSE.

The precip dried out yesterday and we only received about 4mm over the last 24 hours.

This relatively calm period at near freezing temperatures really helps the snowpack to settle and gain strength rapidly. We lost 5-7cm over the last 24 hours due to this rapid settlement.

With almost no precip in the forecast for during the day today avalanche danger is LOW.

From late tonight into tomorrow we will see precip rates increasing adding to our stability equation once again. The precip rates should be low to moderate. The winds are not predicted to be too high... so avalanche danger will start to build again but slowly for tomorrow.

Stay safe out there and enjoy a great day.

Today's Avalanche Tip

This weekend we have some great training opportunities coming up at Eaglecrest which are all free.

Saturday there is a FREE Avalanche Awareness Course that included Stability Evaluations and Companion Rescue Skills Training from 9-330 pm. To Register contact Tom_Mattice@ci.juneau.ak.us.

On Sunday there is a free rescue skills clinic and refresher on how to use your Avalanche Beacon and Probe. This offering goes from 1-3pm. Please meet outside the Ski Patrol Room at the base area.

This Advisory applies to the Mt. Juneau Avalanche Zones
