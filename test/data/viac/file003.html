Sunday March 11, 2012

Date Issued March 10, 2012 at 05:54PM

 

Valid until Tuesday March 13th 2012.

Confidence:  Good. An established weather system is on track to hit land on Monday evening.  This storm will bring heavy precipitation in the form of snow at high elevations accompanied by very strong winds

Weather: 

Sunday: 10-20cms of new snow is expected through the day. Freezing level will hover around 700m. Moderate-Strong winds from the SW are also expected.

Monday: 40-80cms of snow can be expected with deeper accumulations seen on the Western side of the Island (Mt. Myra area) tapering closer to 30-40cms for the East side (Mt Cain, Alexandra Peak). Extreme SW winds are forecasted to accompany the precipitation. Freezing levels dropping to 500m

Tuesday: Snow and winds tapering off with low accumulations of 10-15cms through the day at higher elevations. Freezing levels around 500m

Main Concerns: (Avalanche problems)

Wind Slab - New snow will bury existing wind slabs. Lee and wind exposed terrain will be of concern. On Monday high winds will create widespread windslab in the alpine and into treeline.  Caution on all aspects is advised due to the strength of the wind and amount of precipitation forecasted. Extreme caution is advised in lee zones.

Storm Slab  - New snow and high winds will create a widespread wind/storm slab condition. Skier triggering will be probable. Caution on all aspects

Loose Dry  - Continuous snowfall will create loose avalanches sloughing frequently in un-skiable terrain. 

Snow Pack:

 New snow will bury existing windslabs with  30-80 cms of new snow depending on location. High winds and precipitation amounts will also create widespread new wind slabs that will be reactive to skiers or snowmobilers. Expect to see this condition well into treeline elevations. 

Avalanche Activity:

None reported.

Travel Advisory:

  • Sunday: Caution in moderate-steep terrain keeping in mind lurking wind slabs.
  • Monday: Travel in avalanche terrain or in the run out zones is not recommended. Extra caution in lee loaded areas due to high forecasted winds. Natural avalanches are expected.
  • Tuesday: Remain vigilant after the storm to allow the new snow to gain strength and settle out.  Ski triggering will still be probable.
  • Watch for consistent sloughing in steep terrain during the storm. Avoid ice climbs and areas under or near start zones.
  • Travel will be deep and slow
  • Expect avalanches to run mid-full path.
  • REMEMBER: Alpine terrain example would be the plateau on Mt. Albert Edward, Treeline example would be the top of Mt. Washington and Below treeline terrain example skiing up to Tennant Lake.

Wednesday March 07 2012

Confidence: Fair as snowfall amounts vary widely. Must much more snow on West side and perhaps Northern areas up to 50 cm over night Friday into Saturday increasing hazard to high for all elevations in those areas. Less snow on Eastern Vancouver Island

Main Concerns: (Avalanche problems)

Wind Slab - Old Wind slabs exist in higher wind cross loaded and exposed terrain. By Friday Human triggering is probable or possible in specific terrain features: steeper or unsupported wind loaded terrain.  New slabs could be formed by fresher South or West winds loading NW N E SE aspects.  By Saturday am natural triggering is probable and human triggering likely or certain.  All of these wind slabs are a problem that will be found at tree line and in the alpine and could produce avalanches from size 1 to 3 more than enough to bury, injure or kill a person. Note if any of these wind slabs once triggered steps down to the older crusts it could be much bigger avalanche..

Storm Slab - New snow forecast for Thursday may not come in sufficient quantity depending on where you are on the island, to make for instability. Watch local snow amounts. By Saturday there will be more snow forming widespread storm slab. New storm snow could produce avalanches from medium to large enough to bury, injure or kill a person. Triggering could be likely or probable in steeper or previously avalanche areas or on cooler shady aspects or higher elevations.  Note: If started the storm slab could trigger a deeper or step down avalanche.

Dry Loose: New snow will fall fast and cold. This makes snow pour spindrift down or by the wheelbarrow  load off cliffs and waterfall routes every 10 minutes.

Weather:

The island Alps received cooler stable weather over the week. Mt Cain got another 30 cm Early in the week. The rest of the area saw only light snowfall and very cool over night temps - 9 to 11.  Winds were only a factor at the higher elevations from the West and NW; brisk gusts. The freezing temps locked up last weekend's mess and developed another crust. Some day time warming on Solar aspects.  The Outlook is:

Thursday: Clear and cool day time warming expected with snow starting later in eve. Winds light S.  Freezing level around 900m and rising sharply in some areas to 1800 or 2200m for a short time.

Friday:  Thursday over night snow 22cm or rain West 12 cm Cain 09cm Wash. Clearing in day time and then HEAVY snow starting again in the eve. Winds moderate and strong from the W SW S . Freezing level 900m and declining over day.

Saturday:  Rapid snow overnight cooler temperatures may bump up the snowfall amounts 25 to 45 cm and into AM, more on the West and North island. Winds continue S SW and a bit on the Strong side. The freezing levels 700.

Snow Pack:

Cold snow and or surface hoar may sit on a crust on some aspects. We better hope the Thursday warming  melts it off before the snow starts Thursday.  The snow since late February sits in 4 layers above the Feb 14 crusts. The last wet storm shear has a crust above it now. A storm shear sits 80 down and weak faceted crust is below that and then the strong crust below that nearly 90 to 120 down. All layers are bonding well except where shallower than 50 cm. Steeper starting zone have shed most of the snow down to the mid Feb crusts and are repeating avalanche cycles with each snow fall.

Avalanche Activity:

None reported.

Travel Advisory:

  • Thursday: Steep terrain may have crusts exposed that do not bond as well to the new or wind loaded snow.
  • Crusts may not be supportive making travel and skiing difficult.
  • Enter the wind zone at tree line and above with caution during the forecast period. Old wind slabs exist on may aspects.
  • Friday:  new snow and wind may reload W N E aspects. Elevated risk especially in wind zone.
  • By Saturday. Serious and dangerous conditions expected. Avoid the wind zone and most avalanche terrain. Avoid the run outs of larger avalanche terrain.
  • Generally:
  • Watch for whumpfing, hollow sounds, shooting cracks or recent avalanches.
  • Gradually increase your exposure to avalanche terrain as your information and repeated testing suggest. .

Confidence: Fair as snowfall amounts vary widely. Must much more snow on West side and perhaps Northern areas up to 50 cm increasing hazard to high for all elevations in those areas.

Main Concerns: (Avalanche problems)

Wind Slab - Wind slabs are forming with West winds over night Sunday loading N E aspects and higher wind cross loaded and exposed terrain. Human triggering is Likely or possible in specific terrain features: steeper or unsupported wind loaded terrain.  New slabs could be formed by fresher North West North winds Monday Tuesday redistributing snow and loading Southern SE aspects with new slabs.  All of these wind slabs are a problem that will be found at tree line and in the alpine and will produce avalanches from small to just large enough to bury, injure or kill a person. Note if any of these wind slabs once triggered steps down to the wet storm slab it could be bigger..

Storm Slab - New snow forecast for Sunday may come in sufficient quantity depending on where you are on the island, to make for instability in the storm snow at higher elevations and on most aspects.  These could produce avalanches from small to just large enough to bury, injure or kill a person. Triggering could be probable in steeper or previously avalanche areas.  Note: If started the storm slab could trigger a deeper wet slab or step down avalanche.

Weather:

The island Alps received variable weather over the weekend. Light snowfall Friday at warm temperatures gave way to snow over night giving 15 to 30 cm snow, winds were only a factor at the higher elevations. Rain and above freezing temps developed Saturday until the evening when things cooled of and the snow showers started again. Winds have not been a factor due to wet snow and light speeds. Light snow and cooler temps prevailed for Sunday. The Outlook is:

Sunday/Monday: Snow starting later in the day Sunday and finishing Monday AM. with 20 to 30 cm possible. Winds increasing to moderate W. Freezing level around 600m and dropping.

Tuesday:  clearing and cooling.  Winds rising to moderate and strong from the W NW . Freezing level around Surface to 400m cooler in outflow affected areas EG: East coast.

Wednesday:  Clear and cool give way to light snow in late day, more on the West and North. Winds continue North and switch when the precipitation starts to West to SW and a bit on the light side. The freezing levels rise to 900m or lower.

Snow Pack:

New snow is falling on buried crusts or a wet layer near surface. Wet storm snow from Saturday was shearing pretty easily down 25 on a rime/temperature crust from late Friday. The snow from late February sits in three layers above the Feb 14 crusts. A storm shear sits 60 down and weak faceted crust is below that and then the strong crust below that nearly 80 to 120 down. All layers are bonding well except where shallower than 50 cm. Steeper starting zone have shed most of the snow down to the mid Feb crusts and are repeating with each snow fall.

Avalanche Activity:

Saturday SC triggering of wind slabs and wet storm slabs on challenging terrain was producing numerous size 1.5 perhaps size 2 slides 30cm to with wind slab 60 deep. Mostly steeper slopes all aspects. Big mess and en training more loose wet storm snow on way down. Natural loose wet and pinwheels were observed below 1600 m.

Travel Advisory:

  • Enter the wind zone at tree line and above with caution during the forecast period. Monday with West winds loading NE E and SE aspects. Expected cross loaded and redeposited wind slabs on Southerly aspects. Note that hazard will increase as the wind shifts Monday with cool temperatures to NNW N.
  • Wind slabs are often stiffer and may have pattern, watch for unusual distribution in the forecast period.
  • Avoid steeper slopes and larger terrain.
  • Watch for whumpfing, hollow sounds, shooting cracks or recent avalanches.
  • Gradually increase your exposure to avalanche terrain as your information and repeated testing suggest. .
  • Steep terrain may have crusts exposed that do not bond as well to the new or wind loaded snow.
  • Crusts may not be supportive making travel and skiing difficult.

Important Notice

This bulletin covers the mountainous region of Vancouver Island from the Mt. Cain Ski Area in the North to the Beaufort range to the South including the mountains of Strathcona Provincial Park.

This is a regional forecast and significant variation may exist within the forecast area. The information and danger ratings are intended as a trip planning aid for recreational, backcountry users of avalanche terrain; they are not meant to be used as the sole factor in determining the avalanche danger presented by a specific slope.

Always include local weather, snowpack and avalanche observations in your decision to travel in avalanche terrain. Observations and experience may lead to different conclusions from what is reported or recommended. See disclaimer for further details. The technical data used to produce these bulletins is obtained from a variety of sources, including local ski areas and remote weather resources.
