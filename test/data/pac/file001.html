Payette Avalanche Advisory

Saturday, December 20 2014

Created:   12-20-2014 at 5:27 am
Updated:  12-20-2014 at 5:52 am

Forecaster: Dave Bingaman

Don't forget about our Introduction to Avalanche Class coming up on Jan 2 and 3.  The Friday classroom session is open to snowmobilers and skiers with separate field sessions on Saturday.  For our full education calendar click here.  Brundage Mt and Tamarack Resort are now open, remember if you choose to leave the ski resort boundary you are in the backcountry and should be prepared and equipped for travel and/or rescue in avalanche terrain.  Also, mark your calenders for the Friends annual fundraiser at Salmon River Brewery in McCall, February 21.  

Bottom Line

DANGER LEVEL DEFINITIONS

Summary

The avalanche hazard throughout the West Central Mountains will be Considerable today and likely increasing over the next 2 days. Sensitive soft slabs will continue to grow in thickness rapidly today on many aspects on slopes over 30 degrees. Winds were gusting into the mid 20's on the ridge tops yesterday forming sensitive wind slabs on multiple aspects. The new and wind deposited snow has fallen on older wind slabs on some of our northerly terrain, a variety of old snow surfaces and a layer of surface hoar that was formed earlier this week.  The buried surface hoar layer will become more reactive as a heavy moisture plume continues to add snow today and as temperatures rise throughout the storm. The addition of up to 2 feet of new snow in the next 24-48 hours on old snow surfaces will create increasing hazard on many high elevation slopes.  Use caution selecting your routes, pay attention to changing conditions and make sure you and your group are all carrying avalanche rescue gear.

An early Christmas present is being delivered right now.  Yesterday marked the first day of significant snowfall this month. Local snowtels are reporting between .5 and 1 inch of Snow Water Equivalent through the last 24 hours with another 2+inches on tap through Sunday.  This could translate into 24+ inches of snow by tomorrow afternoon.  Red flags are waving right now and all this new snow means a sensitve storm slab that will continue to build rapidly as this storm develops.  Temperatures are forecasted to rise today with snow lines moving upwards of 6000 feet at times throughout the day creating a mildly inverted new snow layer with soft weak snow below and heavier snow above. Winds will also be a concern as they pick up later today and tonight. Expect to see shallow soft wind slabs on multiple aspects tomorrow.  To further complicate the story, the new snow we received yesterday fell on a variety of old snow surfaces including old wind slabs, firm crusts from last week wind and rain event and a newly formed layer of Surface Hoar. Yesterday at mid day the surface hoar layer was already buried 4-5 inches down and the precipitation intensity increased throughout the day creating a growing slab on this weak layer. (Pit photo with layer of concern marked in red)  We also have a significant layer of depth hoar near the bottom of the snowpack from our early fall storms on most of our Northerly slopes.  While it is unlikely that a skier or snowmobile could trigger this layer right now, if triggered it would be a large, dense and probably deadly slab.  Be aware if you are riding in shallow rocky areas  that it could be easier to trigger this deeper layer.

Winds are expected to pick up this afternoon with gusts reaching over 30mph.  Today these winds will be from the SW creating sensitive areas on windward aspects and depositing additional snow on the leeward sides(NE aspects) as we switch to a NW flow with the next round of storms tomorrow expect to see a shift from SW to W winds and finally NW winds gusting close to 30 by tomorrow mid day.  This will intensify the creation of sensitive wind slabs and wind loading on multiple aspects. Watch out for shooting cracks or hollow or stiff sounding or feeling snow.

Current Conditions

Great skiing and riding conditions on all aspects yesterday, watch out as some of the shallow snow hazards begin to get buried. Off trail travel conditions will likely become more difficult as we continue to get more snow today and tomorrow.

General Announcements
